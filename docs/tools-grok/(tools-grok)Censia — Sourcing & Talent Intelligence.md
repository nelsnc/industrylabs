visibility into workforce skills, enabling organizations to adopt skills-first strategies.

profiles of professionals drawn from over 2,000 sources. This approach addresses

limitations in legacy systems like resumes and job descriptions, which often fail to capture

transferable skills or potential.


Core functionality includes ingesting disparate data sources, normalizing and deduplicating

records, and inferring skills based on contextual analysis (e.g., inferring TensorFlow

proficiency from a Google Data Scientist role). The platform's Inferential Al models career

trajectories, predicts performance, and contextualizes experience (e.g., distinguishing VP

roles at startups vs. Fortune 500). As a "headless" API-first solution, Censia integrates

seamlessly into existing HR ecosystems without requiring workflow changes, injecting

intelligence into systems like Workday or SAP.


Key differentiators include its "Clean Al" ethos — models trained on external, bias-free data

rather than client history — and focus on ethical Al with features like anonymization and bias

mitigation. It supports DEI by expanding talent pools and ignoring non-predictive factors.

Target users are enterprise HR leaders in Fortune 500 companies facing skills gaps,

internal mobility challenges, or compliance needs. Buyer persona: CHRO or VP Talent ina

500+ employee organization, prioritizing data-driven decisions and ERP integrations.
