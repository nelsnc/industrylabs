1. Executive Summary & Basic Information

1.1 Overview and Market Positioning

The landscape of talent acquisition has undergone a seismic shift over the last decade, transitioning from reactive "post-and-pray" methodologies to proactive talent intelligence strategies. In this evolved ecosystem, HeroHunt.ai has emerged as a significant challenger in the Sourcing & Talent Intelligence category. Officially operating under the legal entity Eevee Meets B.V., headquartered in Amsterdam, Netherlands 1, the platform positions itself as a "search and engagement engine" capable of aggregating over one billion candidate profiles from diverse public data sources including LinkedIn, GitHub, and Stack Overflow.2

The platform's primary value proposition is the democratization of technical sourcing. Historically, uncovering "passive" candidates—those not actively applying but open to offers—required specialized knowledge of Boolean logic and complex search strings. HeroHunt.ai disrupts this paradigm through its AI recruiter agent, "Uwi" (also referenced as RecruitGPT in certain iterations), which utilizes Natural Language Processing (NLP) to interpret job descriptions and semantic intent.4 This allows recruiters to input raw job requirements and receive a curated, ranked list of candidates with enriched contact data, effectively automating the top-of-funnel sourcing process.

For mid-market companies (50-500 employees), particularly those in the technology sector, HeroHunt.ai serves as a cost-effective, high-velocity alternative to enterprise incumbents like LinkedIn Recruiter and SeekOut. Its strategic location in the EU provides a unique compliance moat, offering native adherence to GDPR—a critical consideration for global companies navigating the complexities of data privacy in recruitment.5

1.2 Core Functionality and Differentiators

HeroHunt.ai operates as an overlay technology that sits between the open web and the recruiter's Applicant Tracking System (ATS). Its core functionality can be segmented into three distinct pillars:

Cross-Platform Aggregation: Unlike LinkedIn Recruiter, which is confined to its own "walled garden," HeroHunt.ai aggregates data from the open web. This is particularly vital for technical roles where high-quality candidates often maintain active portfolios on GitHub or Stack Overflow but may have sparse or neglected LinkedIn profiles.3 By synthesizing these disparate data points, the platform creates a "holistic" candidate profile that offers better insight into actual technical proficiency rather than just self-reported job titles.

Autonomous Search Agent (Uwi): The platform's proprietary AI agent, Uwi, represents a shift toward "Agentic AI" in recruitment. Rather than just filtering a database, Uwi acts as an autonomous screener. It reads job descriptions, infers necessary soft and hard skills that may not be explicitly keyworded, and ranks candidates based on a "match score" derived from this semantic understanding.6 This reduces the cognitive load on recruiters and minimizes the risk of human error or bias in the initial screening phase.

Contact Enrichment and Engagement: A persistent pain point in sourcing is the lack of direct contact information. HeroHunt.ai integrates a verification engine that finds personal and work email addresses, claiming to uncover "2x more contact details" than standard databases.3 Once contact is established, the platform facilitates automated, multi-step email sequences personalized with data drawn from the candidate's public footprint (e.g., referencing a specific code repository), thereby increasing response rates.7
